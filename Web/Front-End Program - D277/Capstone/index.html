Located in the South Central United States, Texas is the region's most populous state. It is bordered by Louisiana to the east, Arkansas to the northeast, Oklahoma to the north, New Mexico to the west, and shares its southern and southwestern boundaries with the Mexican states of Chihuahua, Coahuila, Nuevo León, and Tamaulipas. Spanning an expansive 268,596 square miles (695,660 km²) and home to over 30 million people as of 2023, Texas ranks as the second-largest state in the country both in area and population. (Wikipedia contributors, n.d., Texas).

(Wikipedia contributors, n.d., Texas in United States)


Texas, known as the Lone Star State, earned its nickname from its history as an independent republic. The Lone Star is prominently featured on both the Texas state flag and seal. Spain was the first European nation to lay claim to Texas, followed by a brief French colonial period before Mexico took control of the territory. Texas gained independence from Mexico in 1836, forming the Republic of Texas.

In 1845, it was admitted to the United States as the 28th state. This annexation sparked events that eventually led to the Mexican-American War in 1846. After the United States emerged victorious, Texas continued as a slave state until the Civil War, when it seceded from the Union in early 1861 and joined the Confederate States of America on March 2. (Wikipedia contributors, n.d., Texas).
